SDGRS is a secure digital layer on top of existing grievance cells. It records every complaint, assigns clear responsibility, and tracks the case until it is closed – with the complainant always in the loop.

How SDGRS handles a grievance

01

Intake & registration

Complaints can come from the portal, helpline, email or offline entry by staff. SDGRS converts each one into a structured record with category, department, and priority.

02

Routing to the right officer

Based on category and location, the system assigns a nodal officer. Internal SLAs define how long each level can keep a grievance before it escalates automatically.

03

Time‑bound resolution

Officers record actions, attach documents, and update status. If the SLA is at risk, the case is highlighted on the escalation dashboard for senior review.

04

Feedback & closure

Once an officer proposes a resolution, the citizen is informed and can rate the outcome or re‑open the case. Feedback scores feed into performance dashboards.

What we insist on every single day

Traceability over blame

The objective is not to find who to blame, but to see clearly what happened. Every step – from intake to closure – is logged with time, actor and action.

Data protection first

Grievances often contain sensitive personal details. SDGRS enforces role‑based access, encryption in transit, and controlled audit access for supervision.

Respectful communication

Every update is written in clear, simple language without internal jargon. The system gently reminds officers if responses are delayed or incomplete.

Learning from every complaint

We do not treat grievances as noise. Trends from SDGRS reports are used to recommend policy fixes and process changes to departments.

Who runs SDGRS

Grievance Cell

A dedicated cell monitors queues, follows up with departments, and ensures that citizens actually get answers, not just acknowledgements.

  • Assigns and re‑assigns grievances when officers change.
  • Monitors cases stuck beyond their SLA.
  • Handles second‑level appeals if a citizen is not satisfied.

Nodal & escalation officers

Each department has a nodal officer for first‑level resolution and a senior escalation officer for complex or sensitive matters.

  • Classify complaints into the right schemes and departments.
  • Record actions taken and reasons for any delay.
  • Ensure that policy issues are flagged, not just closed.

Technology & security team

A small in‑house team maintains the platform, reviews security, and ensures that outages or bugs do not silently drop grievances.

  • Maintains uptime and backup processes.
  • Reviews access logs and audit trails.
  • Updates workflows when rules or schemes change.

What you can expect from SDGRS
