Security & privacy

Data protection

How we collect, store and protect data submitted through SDGRS.

Overview What we collect How we store data Sharing & disclosure Your choices

Overview

We collect and process data to operate the grievance redressal process in a secure and accountable manner. We minimise the amount of personal data collected and retain it only as long as necessary.

Security by design

SDGRS uses encryption in transit and role‑based access to protect sensitive information.

What we collect

  • Contact details required to register and respond to a grievance.
  • Details of the grievance, supporting documents and communication history.
  • System logs such as timestamps, IP address and device information for security.

How we store data

Data is stored in secure environments with controlled access. Backups are encrypted and access is logged for audit and investigation purposes.

Retention

We retain grievance records according to applicable legal and policy requirements, after which they are archived or anonymised.

Sharing & disclosure

Information is shared only with authorised departments or agencies involved in resolving a grievance, or when required by law.

  • We do not sell personal data.
  • Third‑party service providers are bound by confidentiality and security obligations.

Your choices

You may request a copy of your grievance data, ask for corrections or raise concerns about how your information is handled.
